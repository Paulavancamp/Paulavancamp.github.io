Craft

A collection of photos and stories about the construction of original works of art and the restoration of curbside scraps.

Mug Trees

Fall 2018

As the days until Christmas grew smaller, I began thinking of what I could make in "bulk" that many people would appreciate as gifts. An avid tea drinker myself, with lots of mugs cluttering cabinets, I thought making a mug tree would be fun, decorative, and also encourage me to limit the number of mugs I own. I ended up making several for my sister to give as gifts too - some stained and some painted black. After completing 8, I have refined the rig used to drill holes at an angle and can replicate the process really easily in the future.

Adirondack

Summer 2016

This outdoor lounger started with my friend Mary, who wanted to try something new. We followed online plans to start us off, but made modifications to make use of scrap wood from my dad's shop. We started by cutting the sides of the base and carefully sanding their curves to match. Then we attached the front legs and bottom panels. The panes in the back of the chair were assembled to match the spacing in the base. The arms were the the most frustrating pieces of wood I've ever worked with, extremely bowed and splintered, but they were free so we used them to finish the chair.
